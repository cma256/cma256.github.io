- Rich Hickey talks about place oriented programming. - Its rude to paraphrase and he means a lot of things with this statement but put simply its a description of the problems state introduces in a program. You should watch his talk. He's much more interesting than me. - In my case I mean place-oriented programming as not so much an architectural decision but a fact of the world. - The user data exists here in the user table. - I didn't get to pick that architecture but I am required to operate within it. - Place then becomes the accumulated decisions people have made in a software project. - Decisions that are not obvious. - Accepting contracts (higher order functions) in your function signature means you can make progress in the presence of uncertainty. - "I need to fetch a user" is a declaration of intent. - "SELECT * FROM users" is a concrete implementation detail that can be determined later. - It is not material to the logical function of the program. - Though it may have operational implications that you will need to evaluate later. - As do all interactions with the world.
