AI Enabled Development

Published on Sunday, March 8, 2026 - 9:30 PM CDT

Context is a scarce resource. Minimizing context consumption will enable AI (and human for that matter) coding assists to work on our code with much greater competency. When you design-by-contract, when you dependency-inject, your AI coding buddy does not need to know anything about the outside world. It can iterate on the logic of your program with significantly fewer tokens consumed trying to understand the program. This often yields faster more accurate output. AI will produce significantly better refactors. AI will produce significantly better code reviews. It will stay firmly rooted in the logic of your program (until you tell it to expand its scope).

It also has the secondary benefit of reducing the complexity of your prompts. You do not need to produce large design documents to front-load your model's context window before it parses your source code. Instead your source code becomes the prompt. And your source code is infinitely more representative of your program's logic than your design document.

I have found that defining types and protocols and callbacks and at least one exemplar implementation has enabled me to use AI as a mind-reading, deterministic code generation program. And I do mean those superlatives. Given my source code and a one sentence prompt it will produce exactly the code I intended it to produce; without fail.

I have found myself down-grading models because the smaller models work perfectly well for my use case at a fraction of the price.
