The Hunt for an AI Compiler

Published on Wednesday, January 7, 2026 - 9:30 PM

The thought of writing software seems to terrify no one more than software engineer themselves. This used to be an endearing quality of the sorts of odd-balls and outcasts that were willing to permanently damage their own minds to achieve a skill that has a status impossible to communicate to those who can't see it on their own. A software engineer might spend a day or week or a lifetime ruminating on an interface that will perfectly encapsulate a problem that has enraptured their mind. The engineer will find themselves half present in their own life and half engaged in a mental struggle against the abstract. All of this so our functions might look pretty or take fewer lines to write.

The recent and rapid advancement of large language models has prompted an arms race amongst engineers. Skill up or be left behind is the motto of our time.
